Fabricating a Monster: The Orb for Tim Burton

The client, Tim Burton and The Neon Museum, presented us with a 1/16th scale model of the "Orb Monster" sculpture for display in Las Vegas. Given creative freedom with materials, we embarked on a journey to bring this unique vision to life.

Our Approach:

  • Scaling & Design: We meticulously scaled the client's model, creating detailed construction drawings and color renderings for precise color palette selection and client approval.
  • Material Innovation: The sculpture was crafted from a robust steel structure, with foam details meticulously shaped and attached. The final pieces utilized a strong, waterproof two-part epoxy for a high-finish, durable surface.
  • Precision Fabrication: CNC plasma cutting was employed for accurate "rib legs" and teeth profiles, staying true to the original miniature.
  • Integrated Technology: iPad-controlled lighting was seamlessly integrated, designed for a 12-hour loop to meet the museum's specific operational requirements.
  • Complex Installation: The sculpture's components, including its tall legs, were assembled on-site using an extension boom lift for welding and finishing, with epoxy putty ensuring seamless joints.

Project Details
